Nairobi

Nairobi is the capital and the largest city of Kenya. The name comes from the Maasai phrase Enkare Nairobi, which translates to "cool water", a reference to the Nairobi River which flows through the city.

History

Nairobi was founded in 1899 as a rail depot on the Uganda Railway. The town quickly grew to replace Mombasa as the capital of Kenya in 1907. After independence in 1963, Nairobi became the capital of the Republic of Kenya.

Geography

Nairobi is situated at 1°17′S 36°49′E, at an elevation of about 1,795 metres (5,889 ft) above sea level. The city occupies 696 km² (269 mi²) and is situated between the cities of Kampala and Mombasa.

Demographics

As of the 2019 census, Nairobi had a population of 4,397,073 in the administrative area. The Nairobi metropolitan area has a population of 9,354,580.

Economy

Nairobi is home to thousands of Kenyan businesses and over 100 major international companies and organizations, including the United Nations Environment Programme (UN Environment) and the UN Office at Nairobi (UNON).

Culture

Nairobi is a cosmopolitan city with a mix of cultures. It has several museums, sites, and monuments celebrating Kenya's heritage, including the Nairobi National Museum, the Karen Blixen Museum, and the Kenya National Theatre.
